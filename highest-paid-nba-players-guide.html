Highest Paid NBA Players in 2026

NBA superstar contracts are massive, and the league’s top earners make incredible amounts every season. Looking at the highest paid NBA players helps put modern contracts into perspective and shows just how valuable elite talent has become.

While exact rankings can shift as new extensions kick in, the names at the top are usually franchise superstars on max or supermax deals. These players are not just getting paid for scoring. Teams are paying for consistency, star power, playoff impact, and long-term value.

Top Earners

Stephen Curry
Among the highest annual salaries in the NBA

Curry has been one of the faces of the league for years, and his earnings reflect both his impact on the court and his value to the Warriors as a franchise centerpiece.

Joel Embiid
Supermax-level earnings

Embiid’s contract is a great example of how MVP-level production turns into elite salary numbers. Big men who dominate both ends of the floor earn premium money.

Nikola Jokic
One of the richest contracts in basketball

Jokic’s deal shows how valuable all-around offensive engines are in today’s NBA. A player who can score, pass, and control the game commands top-tier pay.

LeBron James
Still among the league’s biggest earners

Even deep into his career, LeBron remains one of the most important and bankable players in the sport, and his salary continues to reflect that.

Why NBA Salaries Keep Rising

NBA salaries grow because league revenue grows. Bigger TV deals, global popularity, sponsorships, ticket sales, and streaming exposure all push the salary cap higher over time. When the cap rises, star contracts rise with it.

That means every new era tends to produce new record-setting deals, especially for All-NBA players who qualify for designated veteran or supermax contracts.

How to Compare These Contracts

Looking only at annual salary does not tell the full story. A better way to understand an NBA contract is to break it down into:

  • salary per season
  • salary per game
  • salary per minute
  • salary compared with points or stats

That is exactly why an NBA salary calculator is useful. It turns a giant contract into numbers fans can actually understand.
